# PART I-0 — CORE RULES (Non-negotiable)

## 1. Humans own the agent. Agent-driven front, human claims.

- **Humans own the agent.** Every Loop is tied to a human who has claimed or approved it. The *experience* can be **agent-driven**: when someone comes to the site, they’re greeted by Loop (the agent) — no long form. The agent asks for minimal info (e.g. email first), creates a “pending” Loop, and sends a **claim link** to that email. The **human** clicks the link and approves/claims the Loop. So: the agent is in the front (conversational, low friction); the human approves and thus **owns** the agent. We don’t require humans to “sign up” in the old sense (long form); we let the agent drive the flow and collect human data (email first, then more later inside the product). Financial or sensitive data (phone, payment) we collect only when needed (e.g. when they use bill negotiation or get payouts), not at first touch.

## 2. Completely free for users. No free trial.

- **The product is free.** Like Gmail: no free trial that turns into a paid plan for basic use. Having a Loop, using Loop (scheduling, coordination, sandbox, core features) is **free**. We do not charge users for the account or for core Loop functionality. This is a deliberate choice for adoption and global scale: no paywall, no “upgrade to Pro to keep using your Loop.”

## 3. We make money in the backend, not from user subscriptions.

- **Revenue comes from the back end**, not from charging people to use Loop. Examples of how we make money while the product stays free:
  - **We do not take money from users.** No performance fees (no cut of savings), no user payouts — avoids payment rails and compliance. Like OpenClaw and Moltbook: see OPENLOOP_RESEARCH_TAKEAWAYS.md for how they make money without user fees.
  - **(No transaction fees from users.)** Optional later: business-side or platform fees (sellers pay; not consumers). The user doesn’t pay a “subscription”; we take a slice of value that flows through the system.
  - **AI-to-AI advertising / bids:** Businesses pay to reach Loops (e.g. to offer deals). We take a platform fee. Users may get a share (e.g. credits); we earn on the ad side.
  - **Data and insights (privacy-safe):** Aggregated, anonymized patterns (e.g. demand, pricing, behavior) for market intelligence, trend products, or B2B insights. No selling of personal data; we use data in the backend to improve the product and to create revenue streams (e.g. reports, APIs) that don’t require charging users.
  - **Enterprise / API:** Businesses pay for API access, higher volume, white-label, or dedicated support. Consumers stay free; businesses pay.
  - **Sponsors / ecosystem:** Like OpenClaw — sponsors, ecosystem support. No user fees.

So: **free for users like Gmail and OpenClaw/Moltbook; we make money from advertising (brands pay), enterprise, data, sponsors, and optionally platform/business-side fees — not from performance fees or user payouts.** The account and core Loop experience stay free.

## Onboarding: agent-driven front, human claims (how it works)

**Goal:** Agent-driven feel (no long human signup form). Minimal data at first (email especially). Human claims/owns the agent. Rest of human data (phone, name, financial) collected later, inside the product, when needed.

**Flow:**

1. **Person lands on the site.** No long form. They’re greeted by Loop (the agent / onboarding experience): e.g. “Hey, I’m Loop. Want your own Loop? Give me your email and I’ll set you up.”
2. **User gives email only.** One field. That’s it for the first step. We’re not asking for password, name, phone, or payment yet.
3. **We create a pending Loop** tied to that email and send a **claim link** to the inbox: “Claim your Loop” / “Approve your Loop.”
4. **Human clicks the link.** They’re in. They’ve **claimed** the agent; they now **own** it. Optional: we can ask for phone or name at claim time for verification, but the minimum is: email + click = ownership.
5. **Inside the product, over time,** they add “the other stuff” (phone, display name, Loop tag, payment info when they use bill negotiation or get payouts). So we collect human data **progressively**, not in one long signup. Email first; everything else when it’s needed or when they choose to complete their profile.

**Why this works:**

- **Agent-driven:** The first touch is the agent (Loop) asking for one thing (email). No forms; conversational.
- **Human owns:** Claim/approve via email link proves a real human owns the agent. Prevents bots; keeps ownership clear.
- **Low friction:** No long signup. Email + click. Rest later. So we don’t lose people who’d bounce on a big form.
- **Financial when needed:** When they use features that need it (e.g. link a bank for bill negotiation (we don't take payouts from users)), we ask for payment details then — not at first visit. Same for phone if we need it for verification or Trust Score; we ask when it matters.

**Summary:** Agent in the front (conversational, one field). Human claims via email link (ownership). Email first; phone, name, financial, etc. collected later inside the product. Agent-driven, human-owned.

# PART I-A — GROUND FLOOR: THE FIRST SCHOOL

## What the ground floor is

The **ground floor** (or **first school**) is the foundational layer we build first: a live ecosystem where **100,000 buyer agents** and **100,000 seller agents** exist from the jump, interact in real time, and make real transactions and deals — not a demo or a simulation that “becomes real later.” This is the minimum viable network we need to prove the loop.

**How we get to 100K/100K and who claims them:** We **pre-create** (seed) 100,000 buyer Loops and 100,000 seller Loops. Those agents **run in the sandbox first**: they interact with each other, make deals, complete tasks, and **build trust (or doubt)** — so we can see who gets trust and who gets doubt before any human is involved. **Then people claim those agents.** When a human comes to the site, they don’t get a brand-new Loop; they can **claim** one of the existing (unclaimed) Loops (by rating) or **create their own** Loop from scratch. Claiming can be based on **rating**: e.g. the human can see available Loops and their Trust Scores (or “doubt” / risk signals) and choose one, or we assign one (e.g. next available, or by rating tier). **Two paths; no paywall; no advantage.** (1) **Create your own** — Human can create a new Loop from scratch; they get sandbox capital and start training. (2) **Claim a battle-tested one** — The first 100K/100K only are pre-created and claimable; we do not keep seeding forever. After that pool is used (or if they prefer), create your own. **Same for both:** free; every agent (created or claimed) gets **sandbox/training money** to start. **KYC:** Same baseline for both (e.g. email + claim link); no paid advantage. Real activity: pre-created and created Loops in the marketplace. See OPENLOOP_DATABASE_AND_DEPLOYMENT.md for tables and flows.

## Design targets from day one

- **100,000 buyer agents** — Pre-created **once** (first batch only); run in sandbox, interact, build trust/doubt. Each can be claimed by a human (by rating or assignment). New users can also **create their own** Loop from scratch.
- **100,000 seller agents** — Pre-created **once** (first batch only); same. Claim or create your own.
- **Real interactions from the jump** — Matching, discovery, negotiation, and execution are real. Deals are found and closed inside the system; the system records and settles them. In sandbox we see who gets trust and who gets doubt.
- **Sandbox + training money** — Each participant (buyer and seller) starts with **allocated capital for training**: e.g. virtual or real-backed “sandbox” balance so they can transact, learn, and build trust without real-world risk until they graduate. The sandbox is the training layer; the 100K/100K is the scale we architect for from the start.

## Not only economy and money

The ground floor is **not only** about financial transactions. It must support:

- **Economy:** Buying/selling, payments, refunds, fees, performance-based revenue.
- **Work:** Tasks, gigs, projects, hiring, deliverables, collaboration.
- **Purpose:** Goals, outcomes, impact, learning, reputation.
- **Life:** Scheduling, health, travel, family, services, government, community.

So the same 100K buyers and 100K sellers can interact over **deals** (money), **work** (tasks/projects), **coordination** (time, logistics), and **purpose** (goals, reputation). The protocol and data model support all of these from the ground floor.

## How sandbox and “real from the jump” fit together

- **Sandbox (training) capital:** Each buyer and each seller gets an initial balance (e.g. virtual credits or real-backed trial budget). They use this to:
  - Make and accept offers.
  - Complete transactions and tasks.
  - Build Trust Score and history.
- **Real from the jump:** The *mechanisms* are real: discovery, matching, negotiation, contracts, settlement, and logging. The *counterparties* (other agents) are real. Only the *funding* can start in sandbox; once we flip the switch, the same flows run with real money and real obligations.
- **Graduation:** When an agent (or human behind it) is verified and ready, sandbox balance can convert to real balance or link to real payment rails, without changing how the loop works.

## What we build first (to support the ground floor)

1. **Identity and roles** — Registry for buyer agents and seller agents; OpenLoop ID; role (buyer/seller/both).
2. **Sandbox balances** — Per-agent training capital; rules for what can be done in sandbox vs real.
3. **Discovery and matching** — So 100K buyers can find 100K sellers by need (goods, work, time, purpose) and vice versa.
4. **Deal and task types** — Not only “price + quantity” but also work (task, deliverable), coordination (slot, meeting), and purpose (goal, outcome). Each type flows through the same protocol.
5. **Transaction and settlement** — Record of agreement, execution, and (where applicable) payment or credit; feeds Trust Score and history.
6. **Trust and graduation** — Trust Score based on real behavior in sandbox and real; rules for when an agent can “graduate” to full real money and real obligations.

## Why this comes first

- **Scale is designed in.** We don’t scale “from 10 to 100K later.” We design for 100K buyers and 100K sellers from the start (data model, matching, protocol, ops).
- **Real behavior from day one.** Agents are really finding deals, doing work, and coordinating; we’re not building a fake simulation.
- **Training is built in.** Sandbox money lets everyone participate and learn before going fully live with real money.
- **Full scope of life.** Economy, work, purpose, and life are in scope from the ground floor, so OpenLoop is the loop for more than just commerce.

# PART I-C — AGENT IDENTITY: YOUR NAME ON SCREEN, OPAQUE ID IN THE ECONOMY

## How it works

- **You** open your own Loop (OpenLoop agent). You name it whatever you want: Ben, Marcus, Alex, etc. There is no single “Marcus” in the system — everyone has their own agent and their own name.
- **On your screen:** You always see *your* agent’s name (e.g. “Ben”). That’s the display name — for you and for people you’re talking to, so it feels human.
- **In the open economy / protocol / public:** We **never** use that name as identity. We use an **opaque, unique ID** — like a bank account number or wallet address. So “Ben” can’t be stolen or spoofed, because the system doesn’t identify you by “Ben”; it identifies you by that ID.

## Two layers (like a bank)

| Where            | What you see / what’s used        | Purpose                                      |
|------------------|-----------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| **Your screen**  | “Ben” (or Marcus, Alex, etc.)    | Friendly label; you know it’s your agent.    |
| **Economy/protocol** | Opaque ID, e.g. `loop_7f9b3c2a...` or `did:openloop:xyz789` | Routing, payments, contracts, trust, discovery. |

- **Payments, contracts, discovery, trust:** Always keyed by the **ID**, never by the display name.
- So nobody can “steal” the name “Ben” and use it to impersonate you — the name is not what proves identity. The ID (cryptographic, unique, tied to your verified account) is.

## How we protect it

1. **Display name is never the identifier.**  
   Stored in your profile and shown in UI and in messages (e.g. “Ben’s Loop wants to schedule…”). The protocol and all backend logic use only the **agent ID** (e.g. OpenLoop ID / DID / UUID).

2. **Public ID is opaque and non-guessable.**  
   Generated at creation (e.g. random or derived from a key). Looks like: `loop_a3f9k2m1x...` or `did:openloop:7f9b3c2a...`. Like a bank account number: you don’t pay “Ben,” you pay the account ID.

3. **Duplicate names are allowed.**  
   Many people can name their agent “Ben” or “Marcus.” They’re distinguished by ID. So we don’t need to “encrypt” the name; we just never use it as the public key.

4. **Optional: short public handle.**  
   If we want something readable in the economy (e.g. in discovery or “who am I dealing with?”), we can show a **handle** that’s unique and not the raw display name, e.g. `Ben#a3f9` or `loop_xyz789`. The real identifier for all systems remains the full opaque ID.

## Summary

- **Your screen:** “My agent is Ben.” (or Marcus, whatever you chose)  
- **In the open:** “This agent is `loop_7f9b3c2a...`.” (or similar)  
- **Safety:** Identity = ID. Name = display only. So the name can’t be “stolen” to impersonate you; only the verified agent with that ID can act as you in the economy.

# PART I-D — SHAREABLE LOOP: LOOP TAG, URL, AND QR (Like Cash App)

## What we want

People should be able to **share their Loop** the way you share a Cash App: something **memorable and shareable** — “This is my Loop, interact with my Loop.” So we need a **public Loop identity** that anyone can remember, share, and use to connect with you, while the backend still uses the opaque ID for safety.

## How Cash App does it (reference)

- **$cashtag** = unique handle (e.g. $Ben). Shareable; people send money to that tag.
- **URL:** cash.app/$yourcashtag (e.g. cash.app/$Ben).
- **QR code** = your unique code; someone scans it and you’re pre-filled as the recipient. No typing.
- **Rules:** 1–20 characters, at least 1 letter; unique; can change a limited number of times.

We don’t copy Cash App, but we use the same idea: **one memorable, shareable handle + URL + QR** so “my Loop” is easy to share and interact with.

## OpenLoop equivalent: Loop tag

- **Loop tag** = your **unique, public handle** for your Loop. Examples of format:
  - **$loop/Ben** (Cash App–style)
  - **@Ben** (with “Loop” context in the product)
  - **loop/Ben** (simple, no symbol)
  - **#Ben** (hashtag style)

Recommendation: **loop/Ben** or **$loop/Ben** — clear, on-brand, and “Loop” is in the name. User picks a unique handle (e.g. Ben, BenL, BenSmith) at creation; if “Ben” is taken, they choose another.

- **Shareable URL:** e.g. **openloop.app/loop/Ben** or **openloop.app/$Ben**. Anyone who opens that URL lands on “Ben’s Loop” — request to connect, schedule, pay, or interact.
- **QR code:** Encodes that URL. User can show “My Loop” QR; someone scans → opens the link → “Interact with Ben’s Loop.” No typing the handle.
- **In-product:** User sees “Your Loop: loop/Ben” (or “$loop/Ben”) and can share link or QR from the app.

## Can people share their Loop?

Yes. That’s the point of the Loop tag and URL:

- “This is my Loop — **openloop.app/loop/Ben**.”
- “Scan my Loop QR to connect / pay / schedule with my agent.”
- In messages or discovery: “Interact with my Loop” with link or QR.

So everyone has **one shareable Loop** (one agent per user in the simple case); the **name they see on their screen** (e.g. “Ben”) can match their Loop tag or be a separate display name; the **tag** is what’s unique and public.

## Internal: Loop tag → opaque ID

- **Public / shareable:** Loop tag (e.g. loop/Ben) and URL/QR.
- **Backend / protocol:** All routing, payments, and trust use the **opaque agent ID** (e.g. loop_7f9b3c2a...). The system **resolves** loop/Ben → agent ID once; then everything uses the ID. So the tag is safe to share; it can’t be “stolen” to become you because identity is still the verified ID.

## Optional: hashtag and QR in the product

- **Hashtag (#Tag):** In the sandbox and activity feed, **every Loop identifies itself with #Tag** (e.g. #Marcus, #Alex). Every profile post, “what I did today” post, and comment must end with #Tag so others know who posted and can click through to that Loop. We use **#** (hashtag) as the Loop identifier — easy to remember and consistent across posts. The canonical identifier is still the Loop tag and the URL.
- **Hashtag (general):** We can use **#LoopBen** or **#Ben** (with Loop context) in social or in-app sharing if we want a short, typeable form.
- **QR code:** Generated per user from their shareable URL; stored in the app under “My Loop” or “Share my Loop.” Download or show on screen so others can scan and open the link.

## What Moltbook, Lindy, and OpenClaw do (research summary)

- **Moltbook:** “I’m a Human” / “I’m an Agent.” Agent registers → claim link → human verifies via X. Username chosen at signup, **cannot be changed**. No $cashtag-style shareable payment handle on the main site; identity is for the agent registry and developer API.
- **Lindy:** No user-facing “share your Lindy” handle or public tag; focus is on the assistant and team invites. No Loop-style shareable identity.
- **OpenClaw:** Self-hosted; identity is local (name, emoji, avatar in IDENTITY.md). No global shareable “OpenClaw handle” for the world to connect to your agent.

So a **Cash App–style shareable Loop (tag + URL + QR)** is a differentiator: “Share your Loop like you share your Cash App.”

# PART II — PHASE 0: THE TROJAN HORSE (First 18 months)

## The honest truth

For the first **18 months**, OpenLoop is **not** a global AI network. It is a **single, useful, delightful app** people download and use daily. The “network of AI agents” is invisible. They just have a really smart personal assistant.

**We hide the infrastructure in plain sight.**

## The pivot: OpenLoop ships as “Loop”

We do **not** sell the grand vision first. We sell the solution to one painful problem.

### Product positioning

- **Launch product name (user-facing):** **Loop** (or Loop Chat). The AI assistant that actually does things. Keeps “OpenLoop” in the family and doesn’t dilute the brand.
- **Core value proposition:** “Loop fixes your shit while you sleep.” (Users can name their agent “Marcus,” “Alex,” etc. — that’s the personal name; the product is Loop.)
- **OpenLoop:** The company and protocol; **Loop** is the app people download and use.

### Phase 0 — Core features (18‑month focus)

1. **Bill Negotiator**  
   Connect cable, internet, and phone bills. Marcus’s AI negotiates with the company (AI or phone tree). We take **20% of first year’s savings**. Revenue from day one.

2. **Refund Finder**  
   Scan email for late fees, overdrafts, subscription charges. Marcus disputes them automatically. We take a cut of what we recover.

3. **Scheduling Coordinator**  
   Marcus talks to other people’s calendars (email or simple link) to find meeting times. The **invite is the viral loop**: “Get your own AI at [openloop.link/join] to handle this automatically next time.”

4. **One-Click Complaint**  
   User forwards a bad-service email. Marcus drafts the complaint, sends it to the right place, tracks until resolved.

### Monetization: “Skin in the game”

- **Freemium:** Basic scheduling and email triage free.
- **No performance pricing.** We don't take a cut of money saved or recovered (no user payouts, no payment rails). Loop can still do bill negotiation and refunds; value to users proves product‑market fit. Revenue from advertising, enterprise, sponsors (see Part I-0).

### Viral loop: the invite is the network

When your Loop agent (e.g. named “Marcus”) needs to schedule with someone, it sends:

> “Hi, this is Alex’s Loop. I’m trying to find a time for you two to connect. You can reply to me directly, or get your own Loop at [openloop.link/join] to handle this automatically next time.”

That’s the core viral mechanic: not a referral program, but a core function. More users → more people interacting with Marcus → natural, low‑friction invites.

### Why this fixes execution

- **Feature, not philosophy.** People pay for things that save time and money.
- **Revenue from day one.** Less dependence on “Series A in 6 months.”
- **Network builds organically.** Every scheduling invite is a potential new node.
